# State Tax Rules for NRAs

This reference covers state-specific tax rules that affect Non-Resident Aliens. State taxes operate independently from federal taxes, and many states do NOT follow federal NRA rules or treaty provisions.

---

## No Income Tax States

If you only lived and worked in one of these states, you do NOT need to file a state return:

| State | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| **Alaska** | No state income tax |
| **Florida** | No state income tax |
| **Nevada** | No state income tax |
| **South Dakota** | No state income tax |
| **Tennessee** | No state income tax (eliminated interest/dividend tax in 2021) |
| **Texas** | No state income tax |
| **Washington** | No state income tax (but has a 7% capital gains tax on gains >$270,000) |
| **Wyoming** | No state income tax |
| **New Hampshire** | No tax on wages. Taxes interest and dividends only (being phased out — fully eliminated for tax year 2025+) |

## California

**Form to file:** CA 540NR (Non-Resident or Part-Year Resident)
**Filing tool:** Sprintax handles CA 540NR

### Key Rules
- **Does NOT honor ANY US tax treaties.** If you claim a $15,000 India treaty deduction on your federal return, you get ZERO deduction on your CA return. Your CA taxable income will be higher than your federal taxable income.
- **Does NOT recognize HSA tax benefits.** Employer HSA contributions (W-2 Box 12 Code W) are excluded from federal wages but California adds them back to state wages. This is why W-2 Box 16 (state wages) is higher than Box 1 (federal wages) by the HSA contribution amount.
- **VPDI (Voluntary Plan Disability Insurance):** Shown in W-2 Box 14. CA-specific payroll deduction. Informational for state return.
- **CA SDI (State Disability Insurance):** May also appear in Box 14 or Box 19. CA-specific.
- **CA FTB website:** ftb.ca.gov
- **CA tax rates:** Progressive, up to 13.3% (highest state income tax rate in the US).
- **Bank deposit interest:** Even though exempt federally under IRC 871(i)(2)(A), California WILL tax bank interest income (e.g., HYSA, savings account interest).

### Common CA Issues for NRAs
1. **Federal vs State wage discrepancy:** Always check if Box 16 > Box 1. Common cause is HSA (Box 12 Code W). In Sprintax, select "Income reported on other page of same W-2" when prompted.
2. **No treaty deduction:** Be prepared for a higher CA tax bill relative to income, since the federal treaty deduction doesn't apply.
3. **RSU taxation:** California taxes RSU income at the same rate as wages. RSU withholding is typically 10.23% for CA state.

## New York

**Form to file:** IT-203 (Non-Resident and Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return)
**Filing tool:** Sprintax handles NY IT-203

### Key Rules
- **Does NOT honor most treaty provisions.** Similar to California, no treaty deduction on NY return.
- **NYC residents:** May also need to file a NYC return (Form NYC-202 or similar). This applies if you lived within the five boroughs of New York City.
- **Yonkers residents:** Additional surcharge applies.
- **NY tax rates:** Progressive, up to 10.9% state + 3.876% NYC (if applicable).
- **NY DTF website:** tax.ny.gov

## Massachusetts

## General Guidance for Other States

If your state is not listed above:

### Determining Filing Requirements
1. **Where did you work?** You generally must file in any state where you earned income (even if you didn't live there).
2. **Where did you live?** Your resident state taxes all income. As an NRA, you're typically a non-resident of all states.
3. **Multi-state:** If you worked in multiple states (e.g., traveled for work), you may need to file in each state. Sprintax handles multi-state returns.

### Common Rules Across States
- **Most states do NOT honor US tax treaties.** Assume no treaty benefit on your state return unless you've specifically verified otherwise.
- **State returns use FEDERAL AGI as a starting point** and adjust from there. Since your federal AGI already includes treaty deductions, your state may add them back.
- **HSA recognition varies:** California and New Jersey do NOT recognize HSA benefits. Most other states follow the federal treatment. Check your specific state.
- **FICA exemption does NOT affect state taxes.** You may be exempt from federal FICA but still owe state disability insurance or other state payroll taxes.

### How to Determine Your State
- Check your W-2 Box 15 (State) and Box 16 (State wages) — this tells you which state(s) your employer reported income to.
- If Box 15 shows a state abbreviation and Box 16 has a non-zero amount, you likely need to file in that state.
- If you moved between states during the year, you may need to file as a part-year resident in each state.

### State Filing Through Sprintax
- Sprintax supports state returns for all 50 states.
- State returns are generated based on the information from your federal return.
- State filing is typically an add-on ($20-50 per state on top of the federal filing fee).
- Sprintax will automatically adjust for treaty non-recognition and other state-specific rules.
