# Tax Treaty Reference — Student/Trainee Benefits by Country

This reference covers the most common treaty benefits available to NRA students and trainees (F-1/J-1 visa holders). All treaty benefits require filing **Form 8833** (Treaty-Based Return Position Disclosure).

**Last verified: Tax year 2025 (filing in 2026). Updated March 2026.** Treaty provisions can change — verify current status at irs.gov/individuals/international-taxpayers/tax-treaties.

## Important Notes

### Treaty Benefits Apply to Federal Only
- **Most states do NOT honor treaty benefits.** This means you get the treaty deduction on your 1040-NR but NOT on your state return.
- **States that do NOT honor treaties:** California, New York, Massachusetts, New Jersey, Connecticut, and most others.
- **Some states partially honor treaties:** Check your specific state's rules in `state-rules.md`.
- Result: Your state taxable income will be higher than your federal taxable income by the treaty deduction amount.

### Form 8833 Is Mandatory
- You MUST file Form 8833 to claim any treaty benefit.
- Failure to file Form 8833 can result in the treaty benefit being denied entirely.
- Sprintax generates Form 8833 automatically when you indicate your country and claim treaty benefits.
- The form requires: treaty country, treaty article number, the provision being claimed, and the amount of income affected.

### Cumulative Time Limits
- Some treaties limit benefits to a specific number of years or months of US presence (cumulative across all years, not just the current year).
- **India:** Article 21 benefits ($15,750 (2025)) are available as long as you remain a student or trainee. Once on OPT (post-completion), you may still qualify if you were a student at any point.
- **China:** Article 20(c) benefits generally available for up to 5 years.
- Track your treaty months/years across tax years. Sprintax asks about prior treaty claims to help with this.

### Treaty Benefits vs OPT Status
- Many students wonder if they still qualify for treaty benefits while on OPT (Optional Practical Training).
- **India (Article 21):** Generally still available during OPT period, as the purpose of OPT is training related to your field of study.
- **China (Article 20):** May still apply during OPT if the employment is necessary for maintenance while studying.
- This is a gray area — if your treaty benefit claim is large, consult a tax professional.

### Dividend and Interest Treaty Rates
- Separate from the student/trainee benefits above, treaties also set reduced rates for investment income:

| Country | Dividend Rate | Interest Rate | Notes |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| India | 25% | 15% | Default NRA rate is 30% |
| China | 10% | 10% | Significantly reduced |
| South Korea | 15% | 12% | |
| Germany | 15% | 0% | Interest fully exempt under treaty |
| Canada | 15% | 0% | Interest generally exempt under treaty |
| UK | 15% | 0% | Interest fully exempt under treaty |
| Japan | 10% | 10% | |
| France | 15% | 0% | Interest fully exempt under treaty |

- These rates apply to the 1042-S withholding rate (Box 3b).
- If your broker withheld at 30% but your treaty rate is lower, the difference will be refunded when you file.

### Countries with NO Treaty or NO Student Benefits
If your country is not listed above or shows "None," you have no student treaty benefit. You will:
- Pay tax on all US-source income at standard NRA rates
- Still file Form 1040-NR and Form 8843 (if F-1/J-1)
- Still benefit from the NRA bank deposit interest exemption (IRC 871(i)(2)(A)) — this is a US law provision, not a treaty benefit
